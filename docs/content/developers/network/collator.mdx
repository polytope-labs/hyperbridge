# Becoming a Collator

This guide outlines the complete process of becoming a Collator on the Hyperbridge network.
Our system rewards active and reliable network participants — Relayers who deliver cross-chain messages and Provers who generate consensus proofs — by converting their earnings into a reputation score. This reputation is the primary factor in Collator selection. By becoming a Collator, you play a direct role in the network's liveness guarantees and block production.

## Who is a Collator

Collators are block producers for the Hyperbridge blockchain. They are essential for keeping the chain live and processing transactions, and
they double as the network's fraud-detection layer for the L2s Hyperbridge messages with: every collator runs an in-node fisherman task that
watches each connected L2 across multiple independent RPC providers and submits a veto when those providers disagree about L2 state at a
height Hyperbridge is about to commit to.
For their vital contributions, Collators are directly rewarded from the network Treasury. Each successful block authored earns the Collator `0.7 $BRIDGE`.
This creates a consistent and reliable revenue stream, with a total of approximately `4,000,000 $BRIDGE` allocated annually for Collator rewards,
distributed among the active set based on their block production performance.

The primary responsibilities for Collators on Hyperbridge are:

- **Collecting Transactions**: They gather transactions from users.
- **Producing Blocks**: They ensure the production of blocks.
- **Submitting to Polkadot Validators**: They submit blocks along with proof to the relay chain validators, in this case, Polkadot.
- **Running the Fisherman Task**: Every collator runs an in-node fisherman that monitors each supported L2 across at least two distinct RPC
  providers. When the providers reach a supermajority on an L2 state that disagrees with what Hyperbridge is about to commit, the fisherman
  signs a `pallet-fishermen.veto_state_commitment` extrinsic with the collator's AURA key. The collator binary will not start without a valid
  tesseract config (see Step 2, Action 4).

## The Path from Operator to Collator

In the Hyperbridge ecosystem, the path to becoming a Collator begins with being a successful network operator. There are three ways to build the reputation required:

- **Messaging Relayers** deliver cross-chain messages between connected chains and earn `$BRIDGE` rewards through `pallet-messaging-fees`.
- **Consensus Relayers** submit consensus proofs for connected chains (e.g. Ethereum, BSC) and earn `$BRIDGE` rewards through `pallet-consensus-incentives`.
- **BEEFY Provers** generate and submit BEEFY consensus proofs that attest to the finality of Hyperbridge's own parachain state, earning `$BRIDGE` rewards through `pallet-beefy-consensus-proofs`.

All three roles earn reputation on equal footing. The Collator set is exclusively selected from the pool of operators who have accrued reputation through any combination of these activities. This creates a meritocratic system where the most dedicated and effective network participants are promoted to roles with greater responsibility and rewards.

## Core Concepts: Stash, Controller, and Reputation

Before diving into the steps required, it is important to understand the two-account model and the tokens involved:

- **Stash Account (Cold)**: This holds your `$BRIDGE` bond and is ideally kept offline in a "cold" wallet for maximum security.
It is used only for high-value transactions like bonding and designating a controller. The Stash account registers as a candidate
but does **not** need to set session keys — that's the Controller's job.
- **Controller Account (Hot)**: This is your "operational" account. It's the account you use for day-to-day operations like running a relayer, prover, and collator node.
    - It's the account that accrues Reputation.
    - It's the account that sets Session Keys (required for block production).
    - This is the account you use as your relayer or prover signer.
    - It must be linked to a Stash via `collatorManager.register` before the Stash can be selected as a Collator.

**Why Two Accounts?** This separation allows you to keep your bonded funds secure in a cold wallet while using a hot wallet for
operational duties. If your Controller is compromised, an attacker cannot access your bonded funds — they can only perform
collator operations. You can always change your Controller using `collatorManager.setController` from your Stash account.

### The Tokens

- **The Hyperbridge Token (`$BRIDGE`)**: This is the primary token earned as a reward for operating network infrastructure. It is a real, transferable asset that reflects your earnings.
    - Your Stash account must bond `$BRIDGE` to become a candidate.
    - Your Controller account receives `$BRIDGE` rewards for producing blocks.
    - Your Controller account earns `$BRIDGE` for relaying messages, submitting consensus proofs, or generating BEEFY proofs.

- **The Reputation Asset**: This is a special, non-transferable asset that acts as a reputation score. It mirrors your `$BRIDGE` earnings at a 1:1 ratio. For every `$BRIDGE` token you earn through any of the three operator roles (messaging relayer, consensus relayer, or BEEFY prover), an equal amount of Reputation Asset is automatically minted to your Controller account.

**Key Insight**: Your reputation score is directly tied to your success as a network operator. The more messages you relay, consensus proofs you submit, or BEEFY proofs you generate, the more `$BRIDGE` you earn, the higher your reputation score becomes, and the better your chances of being selected as a Collator.

## The Path to Becoming a Collator

The process involves four main stages: accruing reputation, preparing your node, linking your controller, and bonding as a candidate.

### Step 1: Accrue Reputation with Your Controller Account

This is the foundation of the entire system. The primary goal is to be an active and reliable network operator. You can build reputation through any combination of:

- **[Running a Messaging Relayer](/developers/network/messaging-relayer)** — Deliver cross-chain messages between connected chains.
- **[Running a Consensus Relayer](/developers/network/consensus-relayer)** — Submit consensus proofs for connected EVM and Substrate chains.
- **[Running a BEEFY Prover](/developers/network/prover)** — Generate and submit BEEFY consensus proofs for Hyperbridge parachain finality.

**Why this model?** Hyperbridge's Collator selection is based on proven economic activity. By rewarding operators with reputation, we ensure that Collators are experienced, active, and invested participants. This meritocratic system ensures liveness guarantees are upheld by selecting for proven value creation.

**Action**: Operate one or more of the above roles using a dedicated Controller account as the signer.

**Outcome**: For every `$BRIDGE` reward your Controller account receives, the corresponding pallet (`pallet-messaging-fees`, `pallet-consensus-incentives`, or `pallet-beefy-consensus-proofs`) automatically mints an equivalent amount of Reputation Asset to that same Controller account. You can check your Reputation Asset balance in the Polkadot-JS UI by going to `Developer > Chain State > Assets > Account`.

### Step 2: Prepare Your Collator Node and Session Keys

To be a Collator, you must run a full node and have a set of hot keys known as session keys available in your node's keystore. These keys are used for signing blocks.

#### A Note on Key Types

You will be working with two types of keys:

- **Controller Account Key (sr25519)**: This is your main account key known as the Controller account. It holds your reputation and is used to sign transactions. This is the same key you use as the `signer` field in your relayer or prover configuration.
- **Session Key (aura)**: This is a "hot" key that lives on your collator node and is used for the high-frequency task of signing blocks.

**Generating a secure Account Key (if you don't have one)**: Your operator's signing key will become your main Collator account.
You can generate a new, secure `sr25519` key for this purpose using the `subkey` tool.

1. **Install Subkey**: if you don't have it, install it with the following command:

```
cargo install subkey
```

2. **Generate a Key**: Run the following command to generate a new key:

```
subkey generate --scheme sr25519
```

  <img src="/sample_subscan_generated.png" alt="Sample Key Generation with subscan  " />

3.**Securely Store Your Keys**: The command will output a mnemonic phrase, a private key, and a public address. Securely backup your mnemonic phrase.
This is the master key to your account. The hexadecimal private key is what you will use as the signer in your relayer or prover configuration.

**Action 1: Set up a Collator Node**: You must have a fully synced node running the hyperbridge binary with --collator flag.
Ensure it is stable and has a reliable network connection.

**Action 2: Generate Session Keys**: On your running collator node, you will need to generate your session keys by making an `author_rotateKeys` RPC call.
You can do this with a curl command:


```bash lineNumbers
curl -H "Content-Type: application/json" -d '{"id":1, "jsonrpc":"2.0", "method": "author_rotateKeys", "params":[]}' http://localhost:9990
```

<figure>
  <img src="/rotate_keys.png" alt="Author rotate keys" />
  <figcaption style={{ textAlign: "center" }}>Result showing when you rotate keys</figcaption>
</figure>


Note: Replace 9990 with your node's RPC port if it is different.
This command will return a long hex string. This is your session key public key.

**Action 3: Link your keys**: You must now tell the blockchain that your Controller account (`AccountId`) will use these new session keys for collating.
You do this by calling the `session.setKeys` extrinsic from your Controller account.

- Navigate to `Developer -> Extrinsics` in the Polkadot UI.
- Select your Controller account (the one with the reputation score).
    Note that this must be the same account configured as the `signer` in your relayer or prover setup — the account that has been accruing reputation.
- Choose the `session` pallet and the `setKeys` extrinsic.
- Paste the entire hex string from the previous step into the `keys` field.
- Leave the `proof` field empty (0x0).
- Sign and submit the transaction.

**Action 4: Provide a Tesseract Config for the Fisherman Task**:

Every collator must run the fisherman task. The fisherman watches each connected L2 across multiple independent RPC providers
and submits a `pallet-fishermen.veto_state_commitment` extrinsic when those providers disagree about the L2 state at a finalized
height. This is what protects the network from a faulty or compromised consensus client trying to commit fraudulent L2 state to
Hyperbridge. The collator binary will refuse to start without a valid config.

Pass the same toml shape the relayer consumes via `--tesseract-config`:

```bash
./target/release/hyperbridge --collator --tesseract-config /etc/hyperbridge/tesseract.toml ...
```

The collator path layers stricter rules on top of the relayer's parser. Anything that breaks one of these rules fails the node at
startup, before block production:

1. **Every supported L2 must have a `[<chain>]` section.** If you configure even one chain from a set, you must configure all of
   them. Partial coverage is rejected.

   - Mainnet: Arbitrum (42161), Base (8453), Optimism (10), Unichain (130), Soneium (1868).
   - Testnet: Arbitrum Sepolia (421614), Optimism Sepolia (11155420), Base Sepolia (84532).

2. **Each L2 needs at least two `rpc_urls`, and they must come from distinct providers.** The fisherman computes a supermajority
   quorum (`floor(2/3 * N) + 1`) across the listed RPCs, so duplicate endpoints add no safety. The validator extracts the host
   portion of each URL and rejects configs that list two URLs on the same host. Use endpoints from genuinely different vendors
   (for example one Alchemy endpoint and one Infura endpoint), not two API keys against the same provider.

3. **You do not need to set `[hyperbridge].signer`.** The fisherman signs its veto extrinsics with the AURA key already loaded in
   your collator's keystore. Anything you put in `[hyperbridge].signer` is ignored by the fisherman path. The relayer signer (if
   you also run a relayer on the same node) is unaffected.

A minimal mainnet collator config looks like this:

```toml lineNumbers
[hyperbridge]
type = "grandpa"

[hyperbridge.grandpa]
rpc = "wss://localhost:443"
slot_duration = 6000
para_ids = [4009]

[hyperbridge.substrate]
rpc_ws = "wss://localhost:443"
state_machine = "POLKADOT-4009"

# Each of the five mainnet L2s must be present, with at least two
# distinct-host rpc_urls. Hosts that match (case-insensitive) are
# rejected as the same provider.
[arbitrum]
type = "evm"
state_machine = "EVM-42161"
rpc_urls = [
    "https://arb-mainnet.g.alchemy.com/v2/<your-alchemy-key>",

    "https://base-mainnet.infura.io/v3/<your-infura-key>",

[optimism]
type = "evm"

    "https://opt-mainnet.g.alchemy.com/v2/<your-alchemy-key>",
    "https://optimism-mainnet.infura.io/v3/<your-infura-key>",
]

[unichain]
type = "evm"

    "https://unichain-mainnet.g.alchemy.com/v2/<your-alchemy-key>",

```

mainnet entries. Mixing the two sets in one config is supported, but a partial set on either side is rejected.

**Final Action: Prepare Your Node for Block Production**:
After you have successfully registered as a candidate, you should restart your node with both the `--collator` and
`--tesseract-config` flags. This prepares your node to start producing blocks as soon as you are selected for the active set, and
brings the fisherman task online so the node is contributing to L2 fraud detection from the moment it joins the set.

### Step 3: Link your Controller to your Stash

You must now create the on-chain link between your Stash account and your operational Controller account. This must be done before registering as a candidate, as `ValidatorRegistration` requires the controller to be linked first.
**Action**: Call the collatorManager.register extrinsic

- Navigate to `Developer -> Extrinsics` in the Polkadot-JS UI.
- Select your Stash account.
- Choose the `collatorManager` pallet and the `register` extrinsic.
- In the `controller` field, select your `Controller` account (the one with the reputation and session keys).

This tells the network: "Designate this Controller to perform collator duties on behalf of my Stash account."

### Step 4: Register as a Candidate By Bonding Funds from your Stash Account

Now that the node is ready, your keys are linked, and your controller is registered, you can declare your intention to become a Collator.

**Candidacy Bond**: To register as a candidate, your stash account must have a `$BRIDGE` token of at least the minimum candidacy bond, which is 200,000 $BRIDGE.
**Action**: Call the `collatorSelection.registerAsCandidate` extrinsic.

- Navigate to `Developer -> Extrinsics` in the Polkadot-JS UI.
- Select your Stash account (the same one from Step 3).
- Choose the `collatorSelection` pallet and the `registerAsCandidate` extrinsic.
- Sign and submit the transaction.

This transaction will lock your bond and add your Stash account to the candidate pool.

## The Selection Process & What Happens Next

At the beginning of each new session, `pallet-collator-manager` runs its selection logic:

- It fetches the list of all bonded candidates (Stash accounts) from `pallet-collator-selection`.
- For each candidate, it uses the `Controller` storage map to find the linked Controller account.
- It validates that the Controller has registered valid session keys via `pallet-session`.
- It ranks the remaining candidates from highest to lowest based on their Controller's Reputation Asset balance.
- It selects the top candidates to fill the available collator slots for the new session.


If you are selected:

- Your Controller's Reputation Asset balance will be burned. This is somewhat like your stake for the session.
- Your node (run by your Controller) will begin authoring and producing blocks after 2 rounds (sessions). To do this, you must ensure that your node is running (or has been restarted) with the `--collator` flag.
- As you continue operating your relayer or prover, your Controller will keep earning `$BRIDGE` rewards and your Reputation Asset will start increasing again, allowing you to compete for a spot in future sessions.

If you are not selected:

- You remain in the candidate pool.
- Continue operating your relayer or prover to increase your Reputation Asset balance and improve your chances of being selected in the next session.

This creates a dynamic and meritocratic system where the most active and successful participants are rewarded with the responsibility of securing the network.
